# Hospital Performance Dashboard (Microsoft Excel)

## Overview

The Hospital Performance Dashboard is an interactive Microsoft Excel project developed to analyze and monitor key operational metrics within a hospital environment. The dashboard consolidates patient information into a centralized reporting interface, enabling users to evaluate hospital performance through clear and actionable visualizations.

Using Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, Slicers, and KPI cards, the dashboard transforms raw healthcare data into meaningful insights that support operational monitoring and data-driven decision-making.

---

## Business Problem

Healthcare organizations generate large volumes of operational data every day. Without an efficient reporting system, monitoring patient flow, waiting times, admissions, and departmental workload becomes difficult and time-consuming.

This project addresses that challenge by providing an interactive dashboard that summarizes essential hospital performance metrics, helping decision-makers quickly identify trends and evaluate operational efficiency.

---

## Objectives

The objectives of this project are to:

## Dashboard Features

The dashboard provides interactive analysis through:

* KPI cards for high-level performance monitoring
* Monthly and yearly filtering using slicers
* Admission status analysis
* Patient age group distribution
* Department referral analysis
* Gender distribution
* On-time versus delayed patient checkups
* Interactive Pivot Charts and Pivot Tables

---

## Business Insights

The analysis revealed several important operational insights:

* A total of 513 patients were treated during the selected reporting period.
* Patients waited an average of 36.3 minutes before receiving care, highlighting opportunities to improve service efficiency.
* The average patient satisfaction score was 4.96 out of 10, indicating that patient experience could be enhanced through operational improvements.
* Approximately 52% of patients required hospital admission, while 48% completed treatment without admission.
* General Practice handled the highest number of referrals, demonstrating consistently high demand for primary healthcare services.
* Patient volume varied across different age groups, providing valuable demographic insights for healthcare planning.
* Daily patient activity fluctuated throughout the reporting period, emphasizing the importance of effective staffing and resource allocation.
* Monitoring waiting times alongside patient satisfaction enables hospital management to identify operational bottlenecks and improve service quality.

---

## Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actions may improve operational performance:

* Reduce patient waiting times through improved scheduling and workflow optimization.
* Investigate factors affecting patient satisfaction and implement targeted service improvements.
* Allocate additional staff during periods of high patient demand.
* Monitor department referral trends to ensure appropriate specialist availability.
* Continue tracking operational KPIs through interactive dashboards to support informed decision-making.
